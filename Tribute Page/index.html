Mahatma Gandhi

The leader of nonviolent resistance

Here's a time line of Mahatma Gandhi's life:

  • 1869 - was born at Porbander
  • 1882 - Marriage with Kasturbai, aged 13.
  • 1888 - Reached Southampton, England
  • 1888 - Enrolled at the Inner Temple, Inn of Court
  • 1893 - takes a job with an Indian law firm in South Africa, where he is quickly exposed to the racial discrimination practiced there
  • 1894 - Founded the 'Natal Indian Congress'.
  • 1906 - discriminatory law is passed in the Transvaal region of South Africa forcing all Indians to register with the provincial government or else face punishment
  • 1919–1924 - becomes a leader in the Indian National Congress political party
  • 1930 - Gandhi leads tens of thousands of Indians on a 240-mile (385-kilometer) march to the sea to collect their own salt.
  • 1932 - Under a new viceroy, Gandhi is imprisoned again.
  • 1942–1944 - ho in 1934 had resigned as leader and member of the Indian National Congress, becomes politically active again early in World War II,
  • 1948 - While on his way to prayer in Delhi, Gandhi is killed by a young Hindu fanatic who has been angered by Gandhi’s efforts to reconcile Hindus and Muslims.
